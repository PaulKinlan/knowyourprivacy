, Welcome to the "Know Your Privacy" application.

This application is designed to give you insight into how the data in your profile is available to Third Party applications.

Key pieces of your Facebook profile are available to external applications, this information could be used for purposes that harm you.

Not only is your information at risk by adding an untrusted application, so is your friends data.

Personally Identifiable Information

Most of the personal information that you give to Facebook is available to third-party applications. This information could be used identity thieves to steal your identity. Identity theives don't need a lot of information claim an identity, some of which is available through Facebook.

ConsiderationsWhen adding an application to your profile consider exactly the information that you are willing to let them view. If you combine all four pieces of the above information it is very easy for fraudster to obtain your identity. The more information that you hide from view, the less likely that a fraudster can use your information. If you don't trust the application vendor, think twice about adding the application.
  • It is not possible to hide your first name and surname from Facebook once you have joined the system.
  • Consider using Facebooks own privacy control for Birthdays. Edit your profile and select "Don't show my birthday in my profile". Third Party Applications can no longer see your Birthday.

Time and Place information

The time and place information held on your profile (Education and Work history) identifies to third party application what you have done and when you did it.

This inforamtion is commonly used as an added authentication mechanism for many web sites and other institutions (such as banks).

If you really need your education history in your profile, limit the data to only the places you have worked and remove any information related to when you worked there.

Current employer information can be used as an authentication mechanism by organisations.

Exposing your employment history could give fraudsters an extra vector into obtaining more information about your personal details. For instance, an identity thief could contact one of your ex-employers to ask for a "reference". If you really need your employment history in your profile, limit the data to only the places you have worked and remove any information related to when you worked there.
